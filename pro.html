List of protine that you can take

by VIVEK

  1. Chicken




    2. Chicken is one of the most commonly consumed high protein foods. The breast is the leanest part. Each 3-ounce (oz), or 85 g, serving of roasted, skinless chicken breast will provide you about 27 g of protein and 140 calories

  2. Milk




    3. Milk is a good source of protein, containing about 1 gram of protein per fluid ounce (30 mL) or 8.14 grams per cup (249 grams). Milk proteins are divided into two groups based on their solubility in water: casein, which is insoluble, and whey, which is soluble. Milk contains all nine essential amino acids, which are necessary for the body, and is considered a "complete" protein. Milk protein is also absorbed well by the body, making it nutritionally the best natural source of protein, according to the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations

  3. Paneer




    4. Paneer, a popular Indian cheese, contains approximately 18 to 20 grams of protein per 100 grams. This makes it a favourable source of protein, especially for vegetarians. Its mild flavour and firm texture make it versatile in various culinary dishes

  4. Eggs




    5. Eggs are a low-carb, low-calorie and low-cost source of protein. One egg provides 6 to 8 grams of protein with only 70 calories. Extremely nutritious, eggs are a complete protein and have a rich supply of key vitamins and minerals.
